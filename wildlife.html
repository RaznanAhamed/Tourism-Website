Yala

Yala National Park is the second largest national park and well known for its finest safari destination in Sri Lanka. The Yala National Park consists of five blocks but only two of them are open for the public. This park is well known for its wildlife conservation of Sri Lankan elephants, Sri Lankan leopards and aquatic birds.

Udawalawe

Udawalawe National Park was used for chena cultivation before it was designated as a national park. Udawalawe national park lies on the boundary of Sri Lanka's wet and dry zones is the homeland for water buffalo, elephants, ruddy mongoose and grey langur.

Sinharaja

Sinharaja is the only tropical rainforest in Sri lanka. Hence, it is of international significance and has been designated a World Heritage site by UNESCO. High percentages of endemic birds, butterflys, amphibians, snakes, fish species and trees can be found here.
