The Sacred City of Kandy

Kandy is located between the mountainous and forested interior of the island. Kandy is located in between multiple mountain ranges including the Knuckles mountain range, Hanthana mountain range and Alagalla mountain range giving the city an elevation of 500 meters above sea level. This city lies adjacent to the artificial lake and south of the Udawatte forest sanctuary. Hence Kandy is knows for its calm and pleasant climate.

Climate

With Kandy being located at the center of the country and in a high elevation, the city has a relatively wetter and much cooler temperature comparative to the tropical climate of th rest of the country. Nevertheless, Kandy's climate is still tropical since the temperature is above 18 Â°C on average. Kandy experiences a drier period from January to April. From May through to December the city experiences its monsoon season, during this season the weather is rough and unstable. From March through the middle of May is the intermonsoonal period, during this time there is light rain and strong humidity.

Cityscape

Kandy lies at an elevation of 465 meters above sea level. The cityscape of Kandy is developed in such a way that it has all its administration buildings at the end of the elongated square like land, an artificial lake and a public garden adds to the opennes of the city's spatial organization. Kandy has now grown out to hold Paradeniya, home to the University of Paradeniya and the Botanical Gardens.

Temple of the Tooth

The Temple of The Tooth, known as Dalada Maligawa in Sinhala was built in the 18th century. This temple is built on a base of granite that was inspired by the temples built in the former capital city, Anuradhapura. An array of materials like limestone, ivory, marble and sculpted wood contribute to the richness of this temple.

The monumental ensemble of Kandy is an example of construction that associates the Royal Palace and the Temple of the Tooth (Palace of the tooth relic) is the place that houses the relic of the tooth of the Buddha. Originally part of the Royal Palace complex of the Kandyan Kingdom, it is one of the holiest places of worship and pilgrimage for Buddhists around the world. It was the last of a series of temples built in the places where the relic, the actual palladium of the Sinhalese monarchy, was brought following the various relocations of the capital city.

Parks and Gardens

The Royal Botanical Garden, also commonly refered as Paradeniya Gardens is situated 5 km to the west of the city center at Paradeniya and is visited by nearly two million people every year.This garden is the largest botanical garden in Sri Lanka extending to over 147 acres (59 ha) with 4000 species of plants. The Knuckles Mountain Range which is a world heritage site of UNESCO is also situated in Kandy. Both Knuckles Mountain Range and Alagalla Mountain Range which is known as Potato Range in English are famous trekking sites in Sri Lanka. The Udawatte Forest which is also located in the heart of this city is a protected sanctuary, it was designated as a forest reserve in 1856 and ut became a sanctuary in 1938.
