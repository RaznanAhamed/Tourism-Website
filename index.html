Sri Lanka, also known as the pearl of the Indian ocean has many attractive sites for you to witness. Despite its small size, Sri Lanka possesses a vast number of natural beauty, including mountain terrains, waterfalls, places with diverse climatic conditions and many more.

Why visit Sri Lanka?

Visiting Sri Lanka gives you the opportunity to experience places with pleasant climate, witness flora and fauna that are indegenious to Sri Lanka. Tourists could also witness various archaeological and cultural sites, religious and historical sites. Sri Lanka being an island is blessed with the most beautiful beaches, giving you the opportunity to experience numerous water related activites like surfing, snorkelling, boating and many more. Sri Lanka is in fact one of the best country to spend your holidays.

Religion in Sri Lanka

Majority of Sri Lankans follow Buddhism while most of them are of the Sinhalese ethnic group with minority tamils. Islam is the third most followed religion in Sri Lanka, having first being brought to the island by Arab traders. Religion plays a prominent role in the life and culture of all Sri Lankans. Hence, Sri Lanka was ranked as the third most religious country in the world.

Sri Lankan cuisine

Dishes include rice and curry, pittu, kiribath(milk rice), string hoppers, wattalapam (a rich desert of malay origin) and kotthu. Traditionally food is served on a plantain leaf or a lotus leaf. Middle Eastern influence and practices are found in traditional Moor dishes, while Dutch and Portuguese influences are found with the island's Burgher community preserving their culture through traditional dishes such as lamprais (rice cooked in stock and baked in a banana leaf).

Performing arts

There are three main styles of Sri Lankan classical dance. They are, the Kandyan dances, low country dances and Sabaragamuwa dances. Of these, the Kandyan style is most prominent. It is a sophisticated form of dance that consists of five sub-categories,

  1. Ves dance
  2. Naiyandi dance
  3. Udekki dance
  4. Pantheru dance
  5. 18 vannam

A headdress is worn by the male dancers and a drum called the Geta Beraya is used the keep the rhythm. The above three classical dances differ in their forms of body movement, gestures, the costumes worn by the dancers, the size of the drums used and the sound of the rhythm. The types of drums played in the three classical dances too differ, the 'Geta Beraya' is played in Kandyan dancing, the 'Yak Beraya' is played in Ruhunu dancing and 'Davula' is played in Sabaragamuwa dancing. Here 'Beraya' means drum in sinhala. The Gta Bera and Yak Bera is beaten by hands, while the Davula is played with a stick on one side and with one hand on the other side. The main distinguishing feature between Kandyan and Sabaragamu dancing, and Ruhunu dancing, is that Ruhunu dancers wear masks.
